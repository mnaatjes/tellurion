# Evolution and History of Context Engineering

Context Engineering is a rapidly evolving engineering discipline that captures the shift from simple prompt-based interactions to comprehensive, context-aware AI systems.

---

## 1. Origin of the Term
The term **"Context Engineering"** was coined by **Tobi Lütke** (CEO of Shopify) on June 19, 2025. He defined it as:
> *"The art of providing all the context for the task to be plausibly solvable by the LLM."*

This definition marked a shift in the industry: acknowledging that the success of complex AI systems depends more on the quality and completeness of the provided information environment than on the specific wording of a single prompt.

## 2. Thematic Foundations
As the discipline matures, it is structured around several critical thematic blocks:
- **System Instructions and Governance:** Defining the agent's identity and behavioral rules.
- **External Knowledge and Retrieval (RAG):** Bridging the model's training data with real-world, domain-specific information.
- **Tools and Memory:** Equipping agents with "hands" (actions) and "memory" (persisted context).
- **State and Orchestration:** Managing the "moving pieces" of a multi-step task across agentic loops.
- **Evaluation and Observability:** Monitoring how effectively the model utilizes the constructed context.

## 3. Context Engineering in the SDLC
Context engineering is not a one-time setup; it is integrated throughout the Software Development Life Cycle:
- **Architecture:** Defining blueprints and context maps before coding begins.
- **Development:** Using coding harnesses and "Product Requirements Prompts" (PRP) for implementation.
- **Testing:** Validating that the agent can self-correct and handle complex, multi-step scenarios.
- **Maintenance:** Managing long-lived "persisted agents" that learn and evolve with the project.
